## 01 Overview

As a UX Researcher at Blink UX, I conducted five in-person benchmarking studies on virtual reality headsets and wearable technology for a Fortune 500 client. The work established a repeatable standard for measuring benchmarking metrics in future studies, while qualitative analysis of participant behavior and responses surfaced pain points that informed design recommendations delivered at the executive level.

> Studies uncovered opportunity areas through quantitative metrics — satisfaction, frustration, and confidence — and qualitative responses, delivered through final decks and prioritized Issues Lists.

## 02 Context & Goals

The client needed a rigorous, repeatable way to benchmark wearable and VR products against one another and track improvements over time. Our team was asked to:

- Create and run benchmarking studies that could serve as the template for future research
- Collect consistent quantitative metrics (time on task, confidence, success, satisfaction, frustration) alongside rich qualitative data
- Compare products across both non-experienced and experienced participants
- Deliver actionable insights — not just scores — that product teams could act on immediately

Each study produced approximately 80 complete datasets and ~50,000 data points, giving the client a substantial evidence base for product decisions.

## 03 My Role

- **Moderated 250+ in-person sessions** in Blink UX labs — welcoming participants, maintaining lab procedures, troubleshooting devices, and ensuring complete datasets even when hardware issues arose mid-session
- **Facilitated team collaboration** through Miro boards and structured team synchs to surface common themes and pain points across sessions
- **Led qualitative synthesis** — tagging themes and friction points across ~50,000 data points per study, moderator notes, and session observations
- **Drove Issues List deliverables end-to-end** — detailed documents capturing pain points, bugs, and prioritized recommendations, with timestamped video examples tied to specific session interactions. Delivered at the C-suite level.
- **Onboarded new moderators** with a comprehensive training guide and deck covering study content, expectations, and data-collection consistency — now used as a standard across other Blink UX projects

## 04 Process

### Planning & moderation

As a team, we created and tested a Qualtrics survey per study to serve as both moderation guide and data collection tool. Sessions were in-person and moderated: participants completed structured tasks, then answered benchmarking follow-ups. As a moderator, I stayed detail-oriented on data consistency, practiced neutral facilitation while remaining engaging, and built rapport in a welcoming lab environment. When devices needed troubleshooting, I stayed agile to protect dataset completeness.

### Affinity mapping & collaboration

I built Miro boards for collaborative deep-dives on session findings and encouraged team synchs with interactive activities to organize thoughts and recommendations. Throughout all five studies, the team communicated timeline goals, insights, and concerns through Slack to stay aligned.

### Data synthesis & deliverables

After data collection, I led qualitative synthesis — working through moderator notes and observations to tag themes and friction points. I owned the Issues List initiative per study: friction points, bugs, recommendations, and timestamped video clips showing participants experiencing each issue. We paired these with final decks and benchmarking score cards that told the story of participant needs and product performance.

## 05 Constraints & Adaptability

**Leadership changes:** The team went through multiple leadership and role shifts mid-project. I stepped up within the reporting process, taking ownership of qualitative analysis and client-facing recommendations when deliverable ownership needed to shift.

**Timeline changes:** Contract and recruitment constraints forced timeline adjustments. The team adapted through recruiter outreach and open communication, keeping studies on track without sacrificing data quality.

## 06 Outcome

Across five studies, the Blink UX team conducted 500+ moderated sessions and delivered five final decks with benchmarking score cards. I personally drove Issues List initiatives delivered at the C-suite level, enabling user-centered product decisions on wearable and multi-modal AI experiences. The benchmarking framework and moderator training materials established standards now reused across other Blink UX projects.

## 07 Reflection

This project reinforced that rigorous data collection and warm participant facilitation are not opposing goals — consistency in the lab and rapport with participants both protect data quality. Stepping into qualitative synthesis and executive-level deliverables under shifting team conditions taught me to own outcomes, not just tasks. If I were to extend this work, I'd push earlier alignment on cross-study metric definitions so longitudinal comparisons are even cleaner from study one.
